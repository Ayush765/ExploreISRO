PSLV-C37





The PSLV-C37 mission, executed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) on February 15, 2017, stands as an unparalleled achievement and a pivotal moment in the annals of space exploration. The primary aim of this groundbreaking mission was to establish a new world record by deploying an extraordinary total of 104 satellites into orbit during a single launch, utilizing the reliable Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV). At the forefront of this extensive payload was the Cartosat-2 series satellite, designed to elevate Earth observation capabilities with its advanced imaging technology, providing high-resolution imagery for various applications such as urban planning, infrastructure development, and natural resource management.

What truly set this mission apart was its distinctly international character. Among the diverse array of satellites launched, the remaining 103 comprised nano-satellites and micro-satellites from numerous countries, including notable participants like the United States, Israel, the UAE, the Netherlands, Switzerland, and more. This collaborative effort reflected ISRO's burgeoning stature in the global space community and its ability to serve as a reliable and cost-effective launch partner for a multitude of nations with varied space exploration objectives.

The successful execution of the PSLV-C37 mission not only secured India's position in the league of space-faring nations but also demonstrated ISRO's exceptional technological acumen and organizational efficiency. The mission's resounding success underscored India's commitment to advancing space exploration and contributed to the nation's growing reputation as a key player in the space launch industry. The accomplishment further solidified ISRO's standing as a leader in providing innovative and cost-effective solutions for launching satellites, fostering continued international collaboration and acclaim for India's space endeavors.

Key Specifications and Mission Details

Parameter Details
Mission Name PSLV-C37
Launch Date February 15, 2017
Launch Vehicle Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV)
Objective Set a world record for deploying the highest number of satellites in a single mission
Total Satellites Deployed 104
Primary Payload Cartosat-2 series satellite (Earth observation)
International Satellites 103 nano-satellites and micro-satellites
Diverse Payload Satellites for Earth observation, technology demonstration, scientific research, and commercial applications
Cartosat-2 Series Satellite High-resolution imaging for urban and rural planning
International Participants Countries including the United States, Israel, the UAE, the Netherlands, Switzerland, and others
Cost-Effective Launch Demonstrated ISRO's capability to provide cost-effective launch services
